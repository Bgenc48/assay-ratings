## Why the methodology computed this grade

Weights (governance claimed, so Admin drops 20→15 and Governance gets 5):
Supply 20 · Admin 15 · Disclosure 15 · Insider 15 · Liquidity 15 ·
Track 10 · Holders 5 · Governance 5. The average runs over scorable
dimensions only; overall = min(average, hard caps).

| Dimension | Score | Weight | Contribution | Driver (from the report) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Supply Integrity | 35 | 20 | 700 | A mint path exists; its controller could not be classified automatically (insufficient data, not an open mint) |
| Admin Surface | 40 | 15 | 600 | Privileged capabilities exist; controller unclassified |
| Disclosure Integrity | 70 | 15 | 1050 | No machine-checkable claims (claim-light floor) |
| Insider Float | N/R | — | — | No data; lowers coverage, not score |
| Liquidity Permanence | 25 | 15 | 375 | Dominant pool (`0x6cDcb1C4…`, $31.2M) LP 0% burned / 0% locked — pool was fully analyzed |
| Track Record | 100 | 10 | 1000 | 1,049 days of age |
| Holder Concentration | N/R | — | — | No data; coverage only |
| Governance Reality | 85 | 5 | 425 | See the problem flagged below |

Weighted average: 4,150 / 80 = **51.875 → 51.9**. Caps triggered: C
(unclassified mint) and B+ (claim-light) — neither binds below 51.9. The
51.9 falls in the D band (40–54.9), and D above $1M liquidity is gated to UR.

**Problem found during this review — the governance score is unearned.**
The registry entry asserts `governance: {claimed: true}` with no cited
evidence. No governance verifier exists in the code; when governance is
marked claimed and no probe contradicts it, the scorer falls through to 85
and the report publishes the finding "Claimed governance verifiably
executes on-chain." That sentence describes a verification nobody — human
or code — performed. It should come out until a governance verifier exists.

## What was independently verified during this review

All from Aerodrome's own published sources (vendor evidence, not chain
reads — this sandbox has no chain access):

- The AERO mint function is gated by a `minter` state variable with a
  `NotMinter()` guard — repo `aerodrome-finance/contracts`, file
  `contracts/Aero.sol`. So the "unclassified mint" is real code with a real
  gate; the scanner simply cannot name the gate on its own yet.
- Aerodrome's `PERMISSIONS.md` (same repo) documents the Minter's power:
  "Can mint AERO and distribute to Voter for gauge emissions and
  RewardsDistributor for claimable rebases".
- The Minter contract address is published in the vendor README:
  `0xeB018363F0a9Af8f91F06FEe6613a751b2A33FE5`.
- A team multisig, `0xE6A41fE61E7a1996B59d508661e3f524d6A32075`, sets a
  `teamRate` bounded at ≤5% of emissions.
- The dominant pool's LP being 0% burned / 0% locked is consistent with the
  project's design: under the ve(3,3) model, LP is gauge-staked, and the
  project does not claim a liquidity lock anywhere we found. The finding is
  accurate as stated ("mostly unlocked"), and no lock claim is contradicted.

What this PR adds for AERO (no score change by itself):

- A `minter()` probe that names the mint controller descriptively in the
  report (the Aerodrome Minter contract). Score and cap stay 35 / C:
  naming the gate is not positive evidence that the gate is safe.
- An inert `review: "pending"` `admin_disclosure` claim quoting
  `PERMISSIONS.md`. Pending claims affect nothing (invariant: drafts are
  inert until a human flips them to `"approved"` in a reviewed commit).

## Recommendation

Three decisions, and how each combination lands:

| Actions taken | Computed overall | Letter | What publishes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nothing | 51.9 | D | UR (unchanged) |
| Remove governance assertion only | 49.1 | D | UR |
| Approve claim only | 57.5 | C | C — exits UR automatically |
| Both (recommended) | 54.7 | D | UR |

1. **Remove the governance assertion (do this).** It has no cited evidence
   and no verifier behind it, and it currently produces a published
   sentence claiming verification that never happened. Diff to
   `registry/tokens.json`:

   ```diff
        {
          "chain": "base",
          "address": "0x940181a94A35A4569E4529A3CDfB74e38FD98631",
          "expectSymbol": "AERO",
   -      "name": "Aerodrome Finance",
   -      "governance": {
   -        "claimed": true
   -      }
   +      "name": "Aerodrome Finance"
        }
   ```

2. **Approve the pending claim (do this, after the quote check above).**
   Once approved, the claim verifies deterministically on every scan (the
   only privileged capability found on-chain is `mint`, and the docs
   disclose it), Disclosure moves 70 → 100, and the claim-light cap drops.

3. **Leave `reviewedLowGrade` unset.** Be aware of the honest tension in
   the table: approving the claim while keeping the governance assertion
   would lift AERO to C (57.5) and out of UR — but part of that lift is the
   unearned 85. With the assertion removed, the same claim approval lands
   at 54.7, three tenths of a point below the C line, so AERO stays
   computed-D and stays UR. That is the correct outcome for now: the
   remaining D is driven mostly by insufficient-data anchors (supply 35,
   admin 40), not by adverse evidence, and UR publishes every fact while
   withholding only the letter. AERO exits cleanly when either (a) the
   Minter-contract mint gate gets a real classification, or (b) a
   governance verifier exists and earns the credit back. Only set
   `reviewedLowGrade: true` if you positively conclude the D is deserved —
   the current evidence does not support that conclusion.
