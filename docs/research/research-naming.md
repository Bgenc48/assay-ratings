## 3. Domain strategy for a crypto trust brand (2026)

**Precedents:** the credibility-tier crypto research brands sit on .com — l2beat.com, defillama.com, tokenterminal.com, chainalysis.com. The tool tier lives happily on .io (bubblemaps.io, dune's dune.com upgrade came later) and crypto-native brands on .xyz (paradigm.xyz, mirror.xyz). Capital.xyz famously paid **$105k** for a premium .xyz — proof the TLD can carry a serious financial brand, but also proof that short dictionary .xyz names are not $10 registrations.

**Recommendation by TLD:**
- **.com — get it if hand-reggable or <$2k aftermarket.** You are selling *trust* to journalists, exchanges, and eventually institutions; .com is the suit-and-tie signal and L2Beat (your explicit model) chose it. 
- **.xyz — the correct crypto-native primary if .com is blocked.** Fully accepted by your first audience (CT/degens/builders). Two caveats: (a) short/dictionary .xyz names carry **registry premium pricing** — recurring $100–$3,000/yr, not one-time (assay.xyz is at risk of this; tokenfacts.xyz will be standard ~$12/yr); (b) .xyz has residual spam-TLD reputation in some enterprise mail filters — mildly annoying once you sell paid alerts to funds.
- **.io — fine but pointless here**: $35–60/yr for a SaaS-tool signal you don't need; register defensively only.
- **Skip .ai** ($70–100+/yr): you want "trusted standard," not "AI hype" — even though an LLM engine powers the product, leading with .ai undercuts the credibility posture.

**Fair budget:**
- Hand-registration: .com $10–15/yr (Cloudflare at-cost), .xyz $2–13 first year / ~$12–16 renewal, .io $35–60/yr.
- Aftermarket: two-word descriptive .com (tokenfacts.com class): typically **$300–$3,000** buy-it-now if parked — reasonable to pay up to ~$1,500. One-word dictionary .com (assay.com class): **$25k–$150k** — do not pursue; launch Assay on assay.xyz/getassay.com and revisit after revenue.
- **Total sane year-1 spend: $50–150** (3 TLDs) or up to ~$1,500 one-time if the perfect two-word .com is parked at a fair BIN.

**Defensive registrations worth doing (day 1, ~$60–100/yr total):**
1. Chosen name in **.com + .xyz + .io** simultaneously (whichever isn't primary redirects).
2. **ENS: name.eth** (~$5/yr for 5+ letters) — your audience *will* look it up, and it doubles as the address that signs published methodology hashes (nice credibility-posture touch).
3. **X handle + GitHub org** the same hour you register domains (free, and squatting happens fast after any public mention).
4. Not worth it: hyphenated variants, typos, country TLDs, .finance/.fi.

### #1 recommendation: **Assay** — with a conditional trigger

**Why Assay wins:**
- **The metaphor is the product.** An assay is the millennia-old, institutionalized test of whether gold is what its seller claims — state assay offices literally stamp a verified purity grade on metal. "We assay tokens" is the entire pitch in three words, and "Assay score" / "assay it first" gives you the verb the brief asked for. No other candidate has this density.
- **Legally strongest.** Arbitrary/suggestive for crypto-information services ⇒ registrable and defensible, unlike TokenFacts/TokenLabel, which are descriptive enough that clones ("CoinFacts") could shadow you with impunity.
- **Collision-free in crypto** across every search run (the word's biotech/mining users — Assay Genie, American Assay Laboratories — are unrelated industries reinforcing, not contesting, the meaning).
- **Grows with the company.** "Assay" can rate tokens today and protocols, RWAs, or agents in 2028; "TokenFacts" is welded to tokens.
- **Founder resonance:** Turkish has the exact concept as "ayar" (gold fineness) — the brand translates culturally for the founder's own network while staying fully global.

**Known costs, stated honestly:** assay.com is unattainable (budget-class problem — accept getassay.com/assayratings.com redirects); assay.xyz may carry recurring registry-premium pricing; ~30–40% of a global audience won't know the word and will learn it as a pure brandable (acceptable — see "Dune", "Nansen"); bare-word SEO belongs to biotech forever (brand+modifier queries like "assay score" are wide open).

**Conditional trigger:** if RDAP shows assay.xyz/assay.io both premium above ~$500/yr or taken, **fall to TokenFacts** and buy tokenfacts.com + .xyz + .io the same day (likely <$100 total if truly unregistered) — it is the best budget-shaped name and carries the nutrition-label story unaided.
